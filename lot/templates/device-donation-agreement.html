BACKGROUND

I. {{ circuit_manager.user.first_name }} is an association established in {{ circuit_manager.established_in }} aiming to provide Internet and new information and communication technologies (ICT) to associations, NGOs, individuals, and non-profit groups working towards change, education, peace, environment, development, cooperation, and social justice.

  1. {{ circuit_manager.user.first_name }} participates in the eReuse.org collective management community where participants exchange common resources to encourage the reuse, traceability, and final recycling of digital devices such as computers, laptops, or mobile phones.

Participants federate and implement locally managed reuse circuits. In {{ circuit_manager.location }}, the {{ circuit_manager.user.first_name }} has been created, an open and voluntary circuit, democratically managed where participating agents are ESS initiatives creating a common space to facilitate the donation, collection, refurbishment, and local distribution of digital devices.

The circuits use eReuse’s free software and distributed services to scale, certify processes, and trace digital devices through multiple cycles of reuse until final recycling.

  1. The DONEE, {{ circuit_manager.user.first_name }}, is interested in the donation of hardware for the fulfillment of public and social interest purposes indicated above.

STATEMENTS

The DONEE is an independent non-profit organization that promotes the strategic use of communication networks and ICTs for development and social justice and wishes to facilitate the reuse of hardware by non-profit entities with a social purpose.

The DONOR directly owns a range of computer equipment that it has previously used for the operation of its services and activities. These are economically amortized equipment with no possibility of use by it, and therefore are considered obsolete and are no longer in use by the DONOR.

The DONEE is interested in the donation of equipment for the fulfillment of public and social interest purposes indicated above.

Both parties declare and acknowledge that there is no exclusivity obligation between them.

In light of the above and given the complementarity and coincidence of their purposes, both parties agree to sign this donation agreement, which shall be governed by the following:

CLAUSES

First — Purpose

The purpose of this agreement is the donation by the DONOR of all their rights and titles relating to the obsolete and unused hardware to the DONEE, who accepts it for the fulfillment of the purposes indicated and acknowledges that it has no accounting value.

The DONEE will channel the decommissioned hardware to the collaborating entities, which will refurbish and distribute it to the receiving entities. The terms and conditions used in the selection of collaborating and receiving entities, all of which are non-profit, are defined in Sub Annex 1 and in the third clause.

In the event that the DONOR wishes to make a donation to the DONEE of other computer equipment in the future, such new donation will be governed by the provisions of this agreement while it remains in effect. For this, subsequent annexes may be signed (see Sub Annex 2).

Second — Conditions of the donations

2.1 The DONEE commits to fulfill all of the DONOR’s obligations in relation to the hardware and its use and to notify its final destination, which may be:

  • A. The hardware will be channeled to collaborating entities for refurbishment and distributed to receiving entities.
  • B. Hardware found to be unsuitable for distribution will be appropriately recycled by authorized agents, preferably those established in Clause Three.
  • C. The DONEE shall not, under any circumstances, use the donated hardware for commercial purposes, although it may be given in use (in usufruct) to receiving entities in exchange for economic compensation aimed at covering circularity costs, complying with section 2.2(E).

2.2 The DONEE commits to perform all acts and manage to ensure that:

  • A. Collaborating entities prioritize reuse, repair, and preparation for reuse in recycling.
  • B. Receiving entities extend the useful life of the hardware and, when obsolete and there is no more demand for reuse, take it to an authorized recycling point.
  • C. Collaborating and receiving entities clearly and visibly indicate that the hardware provided comes from {{ donor.name }} for the appropriate social purpose, and may state in dissemination materials that the devices come from collaboration with {{ donor.name }}. The equipment may only be given in use when:
    • The amount of the use donation shall not exceed the cost of circularity services (transport, storage, inventory management, refurbishment, spare parts, distribution).
    • Preference is always given to priority groups, and conditions specified in Clause Three.
  • D. The DONEE may receive voluntary monetary donations from collaborators to sustain circuit coordination; no discrimination may occur for non-donation. The DONEE commits to transparency with the DONOR about these donations.
  • E. The DONEE commits to transparency with the DONOR regarding amounts billed to receiving entities for the use donation (circularity costs).
  • F. The DONEE commits to send a social impact report to the DONOR no later than one year from the donation of the devices.
  • G. Regarding collections, the DONEE ensures:
    • H. Prior to collection, the DONOR must send an Inventory note with details of hardware (Appendix 1A).
      • I. Hardware to be collected must be centralized.
        1. At least 10 items including laptops and personal computers.
        1. With the inventory note, the DONEE seeks a collaborating entity willing to collect.
        1. If accepted, the DONEE refers it to a collaborator who collects everything.
      • V. If not accepted, the DONEE informs the DONOR what actions are needed to make the collection acceptable.

2.3 Data wipe will be carried out by the DONOR, adopting security measures to comply with applicable data protection regulations, including: - Organic Law 3/2018 (Spain). - Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR).

2.4 At the time of the donation, the DONOR allows the DONEE to donate the hardware to collaborating entities; this can be carried out at the DONOR’s facilities and does not require the physical presence of the DONEE.

2.5 The DONOR will complete Appendix 1A and, before donation, formalize the declaration of the devices as unusable goods and their removal from the DONOR’s inventory note.

Third — Conditions of destination / allocation

3.1 The DONEE commits to fulfill all of the DONOR’s obligations in relation to the hardware and its use and to notify its final destination, which may be: - A. Refurbished and distributed for reuse via collaborating entities. - B. Recycled by authorized agents if unsuitable for reuse. - C. Granted in use (usufruct) to receiving entities consistent with circularity cost requirements.

3.2 The receivers are the entities designated to receive reusable hardware. Receivers must primarily be social interest groups, including cooperatives, foundations, associations, non-profit entities and networks, social services facilities, vulnerable families or individuals, or families with minors in school lacking adequate equipment.

3.3 Collaborating entities maintain a needs list; order of arrival determines priority. If within one month after collection there are no pending needs and no new requests from priority groups, hardware may be allocated to the general public, provided refurbishment/distribution costs are within 80–120 EUR. Priority groups always take precedence.

3.4 The DONEE will monitor selection conditions and report to the oversight committee of Clause Six.

Fourth — Certificates

4.1 Delivery note (Appendix 1B): Upon receipt, the DONEE issues a delivery note confirming discrepancies vs. DONOR inventory. It includes: - DONOR inventory identifier - Brand/model - Manufacturer/serial number (for reuse hardware) - Status (pending review / destined for reuse / destined for recycling) - Collaborating entity for refurbishment/distribution and collaborating entity for recycling

4.2 Reuse/Recycling note (Appendix 1C): Periodically, the DONEE issues a list of hardware reused or recycled, including: - DONOR inventory identifier - Brand/model - Manufacturer/serial - Collaborating entities (reuse/recycling) - Receiving entity name (for reused hardware; no individual names disclosed) - Total number of social-interest-group receivers - Amount paid (only for reused hardware)

Fifth — Responsibilities

The DONOR may seek compensation from the DONEE for damages resulting from breach of management obligations. The DONOR is not responsible for operation of the hardware and offers no warranty to the DONEE.

Sixth — Monitoring

A Monitoring Committee will be established to: - Ensure proper development of the agreement - Ensure commitments are fulfilled - Ensure donations comply with regulations - Ensure correct application of selection conditions - Specify which equipment can be donated (including unusable-goods declaration and inventory removal)

It consists of {{ circuit_manager.committee }} people from the circuit and {{ circuit_manager.committee }} people from {{ donor.name }}. It meets annually and keeps records.

Regardless of the committee, the DONEE will regularly present monitoring reports to {{ donor.name }} and circuit adherents.

Seventh — Duration

This agreement is valid for 4 years from the date of formalization. No extension is contemplated.

Eighth — Economic counterparts

This agreement does not imply any economic obligations between the parties.

Ninth — Confidentiality and personal data processing

The parties commit to discretion regarding confidential information. Both parties commit to comply with GDPR and Organic Law 3/2018 and implementing regulations.

Tenth — Termination and effects of non-compliance

This agreement terminates by: - Expiration of term - Mutual written agreement - Failure to fulfill purpose/destination - Breach of established agreements - Denunciation by one party with at least 2 months notice (without prejudice to finishing initiated commitments) - General causes established in current legislation

Any breach allows the aggrieved party to enforce fulfillment or resolve the agreement.

Eleventh — Jurisdiction and applicable laws

For disputes, the parties submit to Courts and Tribunals of {{ jurisdiction.city }}, expressly waiving any other jurisdiction, or to arbitration if agreed.

This Agreement is governed by EU law, supplemented by substantive national law of Spain (except conflict-of-laws rules). Parties commit to good-faith fulfillment and necessary negotiations.

Sub Annex 1 — Selection criteria for collaborating entities

This appendix details criteria and conditions for selecting COLLABORATING ENTITIES.

COLLABORATING ENTITIES must demonstrate: 1. They are part of the collaborative platform Circuit (open and voluntary adherence). 2. They integrate into circuit operations to facilitate effective allocation/distribution. 3. They are a non-profit organization. 4. They are interested in internal use and/or facilitating reuse of the hardware. 5. They commit to fulfill agreement commitments and ensure traceability systems. 6. They agree to comply with principles of public, social, and environmental interest. 7. They distribute equipment to recipients according to the criteria established in clause 4.2 of the agreement.
